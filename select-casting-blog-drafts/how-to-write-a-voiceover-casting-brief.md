**How to Write a Voiceover Casting Brief: A Template for Producers**

As an ad agency producer or brand manager, you know that a well-crafted voiceover (VO) casting brief is essential to getting better auditions faster. But what exactly should you include in your brief? In this article, we'll break down the 7 must-haves for every VO casting brief and provide a practical template to help you get started.

**Why a Good Brief Matters**

A good voiceover casting brief is more than just a list of requirements – it's a roadmap for our casting team. With a clear and concise brief, we can quickly understand your vision and deliver high-quality talent that meets your needs. On the other hand, a vague or incomplete brief can lead to frustration, delays, and subpar results.

**The 7 Things Every VO Casting Brief Must Include**

### 1. Project Type and Product

* What is the project for (TV spot, radio, social, podcast, etc.)?
* What product or service are we promoting?

This information helps us understand your goals and tailor our search to find the right talent.

### 2. Script

* Is there a final script available, or can you provide a near-final version?
* Why is this important? A well-written script provides context for our casting team and ensures that the voiceover talent understands the tone and direction of the project.

### 3. Number of Roles and Character Description

* How many roles do we need to cast (e.g., narrator, spokesperson, etc.)?
* What are the character descriptions for each role?

This information helps us identify the right talent for each part and ensures that our casting team is on the same page as you.

### 4. Union Status

* Is this project union (SAG-AFTRA) or non-union?
* Why is this important? Union status affects our casting process, audition fees, and overall workflow.

### 5. Usage and Markets

* Where will the project be broadcast (broadcast, digital, regional, national, global)?
* What are the specific markets we need to target?

This information helps us understand your reach and ensure that our casting team selects talent with the right level of exposure.

### 6. Session Date/Deadline

* When do you need the auditions completed by?
* Why is this important? A clear deadline ensures that our casting team stays on track and delivers results within your timeline.

### 7. Talent Budget Range

* What is your budget range for voiceover talent?
* Why is giving a range helpful? It allows us to provide more accurate quotes, manage expectations, and ensure that we're working with the right talent at the right price.

**Additional Notes:**

[Insert any additional notes or comments]

By following this template, you'll be able to provide a clear and concise brief that sets us up for success. Remember, the key is to be specific, detailed, and realistic.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid**

* Vague tone direction: Avoid using phrases like "just try to make it sound good" or "we need someone with a lot of energy." Instead, provide clear guidance on the tone and style you're looking for.
* No script: Make sure to include a final script or near-final version. This helps us understand your vision and deliver high-quality talent.
* ASAP deadlines: While we want to work efficiently, using "ASAP" as a deadline can be misleading. Instead, provide a realistic timeline that allows us to plan and prepare accordingly.
* No budget: Providing a clear talent budget range is essential for managing expectations and ensuring that we're working with the right talent at the right price.
